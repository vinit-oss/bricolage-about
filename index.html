Who we are

A team of architects, designers and hard-to-label creative instigators.

Our work is about space, buildings, landscape, art — and especially the people who inhabit them. We operate at the intersections of architecture, design, media and gaming, fostering a collaborative and interdependent practice that embraces experimentation. The studio looks to create a practice that lies on the boundaries of architecture, design and media.

How we are different

Every project is held against three measures.

We hold our projects to the highest standards. We usually pass them through the following parameters for us to deliver value.

001
Socially Conscious
Design that thinks about the people it serves and the wider city it sits inside. Our work is informed by our research on Mumbai, walkability, public space and the politics of the everyday.
002
Materially Relevant
We favour recycled wood over veneer, lime plaster over texture paint, Indian stone over tile. Small material decisions add up to a smaller chemical footprint — and a better-looking room.
003
Digitally Engaged
We use game engines as design tools, VR as a model-making medium, and code as a way of drawing. Architecture today has to be designed for screens as much as for ground.
Our process

Design as process. Process as design.

Every project unfolds through a thoughtful, evolving sequence — driven by research, data and collaboration. At each step we unlock new possibilities, keeping the work materially relevant and grounded in reality.

01 / Listen
Empathise
Hear the concerns, aspirations and visions of clients and users.
02 / Clarify
Define
Frame the brief, the constraints, and the questions worth asking.
03 / Explore
Ideate
Sketch, model, write — in whatever medium serves the idea best.
04 / Build
Prototype
Physical models, 3D prints, VR walkthroughs, mock-ups at full scale.
05 / Refine
Test
Detail, document, validate. Push the idea back to first principles.

Signature work

Selected projects & case studies.

Experiential · Museum · TIME 2024
Museum of Solutions (MuSo)
JSW Group, Mumbai — 100,000 sq ft
A LEED-certified, ten-storey experiential children's museum designed to foster collaboration, critical thinking, compassion and courage. Named to TIME Magazine's World's Greatest Places of 2024, and the first Indian museum to win Europe's Hands-On Children-in-Museums (Miffy) award. Co-developed with Ratan J. Batliboi Consultants.
Hospitality · Bastian Riviera
The Riviera Collection
For Bastian Hospitality — ongoing
An evolving cluster of riverfront hospitality projects for the Bastian group: The Yacht Club at Bastian Riviera, Sir Tuffy — the Riviera River Boat, the Riviera Pyramid, and the Riverfront Deck & Beach. A landscape, a boat, a pyramid and a deck — designed as one continuous narrative of water, leisure and theatre.
Media · Game Design
Khirkee 2027
KHOJ Collective — Delhi / London / Mumbai
A narrative-driven game set in a dystopian near-future Delhi. The first fully-functional art game built by an Indian design studio. Exhibited at India Art Fair 2017 and the Southbank Centre Alchemy Festival, London. A meditation on identity, migration and gender, played through wandering.
Hospitality · Sustainable
Ahvyay Resort
11-acre eco-friendly resort
A resort built largely from reclaimed materials — saree-block walls, foraged windows, recycled timber. Designed as a quiet argument for what Indian hospitality can look like when it begins with the scrapyard rather than the catalogue.
Cinema · Interior
PVR INOX · Bhilwara & JTM
Immersive cinema interiors
Art-Deco-led interior design for the country's leading multiplex chains. Part of a longer body of work on cinema spaces — over a hundred multiplexes designed across India.
Multimedia · Installation
Ambedkar in Bombay
Columbia Global Centers × KRVIA
An interactive 3D map of Bombay tracing the geographies of Ambedkar's life — the maidans, the chawls, the speech sites. Sensor-activated TVs uncovered stories based on visitor choice, making history a thing one walks through rather than reads.
Research · Drawing
Reading the Raga
Ongoing studio research
An ongoing study translating Indian classical music into drawing. Frequencies of ragas are parsed through code generators to produce visual scores — an investigation, after Xenakis, into why certain ragas belong to certain hours of the day.

Global recognition

Two of our spaces have travelled far past Mumbai.

TIME — World's Greatest Places 2024
Museum of Solutions (MuSo)

In August 2024, TIME Magazine named MuSo to its annual list of the 100 World's Greatest Places — one of only a handful of Indian destinations on the list. The museum was recognised in the “Places to Visit” category for its experiential approach to children's learning.

Hands-On! International Award
First Indian museum to win

MuSo is the first Indian museum to receive Europe's Hands-On! Children-in-Museums (Miffy) Award — the world's most respected recognition for participatory children's museums.

Hospitality · Riverfront

Our ongoing work with the Bastian Hospitality group on their Riviera collection — a yacht club, a river boat, a pyramid pavilion and a riverfront beach — expands the studio's hospitality practice into Mumbai's most-watched dining and leisure brand.
